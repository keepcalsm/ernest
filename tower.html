The Pasig Revolving Tower

The Pasig Revolving Tower, completed in 1974 and originally known as the Mutya ng Pasig Tower, is a landmark in Pasig City, Metro Manila. Featuring a unique revolving restaurant, it underwent significant renovations from 2015 to 2016, adding a 12th floor and improving its facilities. Today, it houses various offices, including those for Market Administration and Tourism, as well as the Museum of Local and Modern Art (MOLMA). The tower not only showcases Brutalist architecture but also serves as a cultural symbol for the city, hosting civic events and contributing to the community's identity.
